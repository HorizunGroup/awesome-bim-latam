# Contributing

Thanks for wanting to help. This list is maintained by [Horizun Group](https://horizunhub.com), and it runs on the experience of people who work on jobsites — not on filling out categories.

## The only criterion

**The resource must have served you on a real project.** If you haven't used it, don't propose it.

## How to propose a resource

1. Fork the repo and create a branch.
2. Add your link under the right section, following the format:

   ```markdown
   - [Name](https://url) — What it is and why it's worth it, in one line.
   ```

3. Open a Pull Request and tell us **which project you used it on**. That sentence carries the most weight in review.

## What we're looking for

- Tools, APIs and libraries that are **alive** (maintained recently).
- Official documentation over third-party blogs — unless the blog is genuinely better.
- Resources that solve a problem you actually hit, not ones that look good on a slide.

## What doesn't get in

- Affiliate links or marketing in disguise.
- Abandoned projects or broken links.
- Paid resources with no clear free alternative, unless they're an industry standard.
- Duplicates: if something equivalent is already listed, argue why yours is better.

## Language

The list is in English so it reaches the whole BIM community. Write entries in English — but if a resource is only available in Spanish (or any other language), say so in the description, the way we flag the *In Spanish* items. Don't leave people guessing what they're clicking into.

## About the Horizun resources

Entries marked *(Horizun)* are ours. They're here because we use them daily, and they're flagged so you know where they come from. If you think one doesn't earn its place, say so in an issue — we'll discuss it.

## Reporting a broken link

Open an [issue](../../issues) with the section and the link. Broken links are the fastest way for a list to lose credibility, so they get fixed first.
